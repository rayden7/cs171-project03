Isle of Man TT

The Isle of Man TT is the world's most famous, prestigious, and infamous motorcycle road race (for more details about what the TT is, please see the About page). Our visualization is focused on visualizing rider race performance across all of the variety of race classes (there are several).


The TT race is run on the Snaefell Mountain Course is a notoriously difficult and challenging course, and conventional wisdom is that it takes anywhere from 3 - 10 years of racing on the course to become proficient (this range was taken as a direct quote from both the TT3D: Closer To The Edge documentary, as well as a BBC ITV4 documentary Grand Prix Racer).


The official Isle of Man TT website has a wonderful resource in the TT Database, which we used as our primary data source, and we wrote Python scripts to mine the data. We concerned ourselves with showing line graphs per rider, per race class so that we could visualize how the rider has performed in a given race class. For more information, see the Inspiration and About pages.

Notes on the Visualization

When you mouse over a racing line in the primary visualization above, the line will turn black to call it out so that you can see how the rider has performed in the selected race class over time. Race positions go from 1 to 71. Position 72 (the bottom of the graph) is equivalent to a DNF (Did Not Finish) race placement (riders that do not finish may have crashed, experienced a mechanical failure, retired, or worse). We also show a tooltip when you mouseover a racing line and it shows who the rider is, and the race class the line is for.

We also show other visualizations below when you select a racing line, as well as showing detailed information on the rider in the panel on the right (drill-down & linking functionality). Also, when you mouse over a rider's racing line, we show two (or three, depending on if the rider has at least one or more rce finishes in the top 5) below the main visualization) sub-visualizations below:

  1. Left-most sub-visualization - Number of Times Rider Has Come In a Given Position Per Race Class
    The left-most sub-visualization shows the race position ("Race Position", X-axis) that the rider has come in a given spot and the number of times they earned that place ("Number of Race Class Wins", Y-axis).
  2. Middle sub-visualization - Rider's Average Race Speed Per Year In Selected Race Class
    The middle sub-visualization shows the rider's average lap speed per year for the currently moused-over race class.
  3. Right-most sub-visualization (if applicable) - Rider's Average Race Speed Per Year When Finishing In Top 5 Spots For Selected Race Class
    If a rider has finished in the top 5 spots for a given race class, then this chart will show, and it will display the rider's average lap speed for the currently selected race class per year. The size of the circles also corresponds to the average lap speed (e.g., greater lap speed circles will have larger radii). Since this visualization is data-dependent, to see it you should try mousing-over some of the upper lines that correspond to top 5 slots.

The visualization also lets you filter the primary visualization area based on a given race class; you may select one race class at a time to display, or select all race classes. We have plans to improve and extend this filtering more in Project 3 (for our other ideas and things we would like to change/fix, see our About page.
